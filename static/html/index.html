Forecasting trends in healthcare


The current global pandemic has strained the Canadian health care system to the point of failure. Hospitals are exceeding capacity and many routine surgeries and treatments are being delayed as more acute cases take priority, and the increased stress and psychological strain on doctors and nurses further reducing the capacity and quality of patient care.

The regional restrictions and lockdowns have also highlighted the mental health crisis which carries its own healthcare burden. Mental health disorders have high correlation to alterations in lifestyle such as increased alcohol, cannabis and cigarettes. As are the increase in deaths related to drugs, alcohol and suicide. The growing incidence of depression and PTSD in COVID-19 survivors further presses this concern.

By exploting Google Trends data a general estiamate of precieved stress can be identified. Providing NRT data regarding the psychological state of a general population may help predict crime rates, health trends and other socio-economic indicators.
