The Study

The Framingham heart study begain in 1948 when 5000 residents of Framingham, MA agreed to participate in a long-term ongoing study of heart disease. The residents were between 30-60 years of age at the time of enrollment and underwent physicals consisting of electrocardiogram, chest X-ray and labratory tests.

The Framingham studies are credited with multiple fundamental findings including the relationship between heart disease and smoking, obesity, exercise, high blood pressure, cholesterol, and psychosocial factors. The study is now one of the longest and most important epidemiolgical studies in medical history, and responsible for our most fundamental understandings of heart pathophysiology.

In 1971 began it's second phase, recruiting 5000 children of the origonal cohort along with their spouses, and is coloberating with research institutes to provide better understanding of osteoporosis, nutrition, as well as ENT and lung conditions. The study now includes the third generation of participants and is now also studying mental heath along with the continued research into cardiopulmonary physiology.
