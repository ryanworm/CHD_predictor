Heart Disease Prediction

The prediction of heart disease represents one of the most valuable applications of machine learning in healthcare. The cost to Canadians (both direct and indirect) exceeeded 21 billoin dollards in 2019 and the current crisis only reinforces the need for preventative medicine. By identifying individuals who may be at risk of heart disease, preventative measures or treatment programs can be implimented to reduce the risk of CHD.

The model was trained using public data from the Framingham heart study on kaggle and processed to omit null values and redundant attributes. From an origonal data set of n=4240 pre-processing the data in this manner led to 15.21% data loss leaving us with a sample n=3658. This was deemed sufficient for our non-production purposes. Our dataset only contained 15.18% positive for CHD within 10 years so we upsampled our data to ensure a balanced sample set for training purpopses (n=6202).
Random forest and decision tree models had the highest accuracy on training data but performed poorly in practical testing. The models underfit the data and had frequent false negative predictions for CHD. In order to increase our precision we used k-nearest neighbor. Although a less accurate model, it performed far superior in identifying individuals at risk for CHD.
