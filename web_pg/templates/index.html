Machine learning in health care

The current global pandemic has strained the Canadian health care system to the point of failure. Hospitals are exceeding capacity and many routine surgeries and treatments are being delayed as more acute cases take priority, and the increased stress and psychological strain on doctors and nurses further reducing the capacity and quality of client care.
To help unburden the load, machine learning can be used to help identify high risk patients, such as those who are at risk of developing heart disease. Prediction of cardiovascular disease is regarded as one of the most important subjects in the section of clinical data analysis. Identifying individuals who are at higher risk of heart disease can allow for early intervention with less invasive methods leading to better outcomes at a fraction the cost.
