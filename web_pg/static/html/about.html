Coronary Heart Disease

Coronary heart disease (CHD) or coronary artery disease (CAD) is the most common type of cardiovascular disease. CHD occurs when one of more of the coronary arteries that supply the heart muscle become diseased or damaged due to plaque deposits and peripheral resistance. Inflammation and plaque cause a hardening and thickening of the vascular wall, a process commonly referred to as atherosclerosis.

The three main types of coronary artery disease are:

    Obstructive - Significant narrowing of the blood vessel
    Non-obstructive - Blood vessels divaricating due to increased blood pressure and compressive forces of cardiac muscle. This results in more numerous smaller vessels increasing peripheral resistance
    Spontaneous coronary artery dissection (SCAD) - Tearing of the blood vessels in the heart
