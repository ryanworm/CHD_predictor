Methods

The model was trained using public data from the Framingham heart study on kaggle and processed to omit null values and redundant attributes. From an origonal data set of n=4240 pre-processing the data in this manner led to 15.21% data loss leaving us with a sample n=3658. This was deemed sufficient for our non-production purposes. Our dataset only contained 15.18% positive for CHD within 10 years so we upscaled our data to ensure a balanced sample for training purpopses (n=6202).
Training multiple types of models we chose to go with a random forest model as it gave us the highest F1 (accuracy) value of 0.96. This may result in slight over fitting, but for as it gave us the best predictive value for both negative and positive cases we decided to utilize it for now.
